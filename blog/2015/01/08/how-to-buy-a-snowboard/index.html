How to Buy a Snowboard

Jan 8th, 2015 11:47 pm

If you are reading this article - you are complete noob. So, DO NOT BUY a snowboard, buy pair of snowboard boots! This is the most important gear. You are going to wear them 6-7 hours a day. Go to the store, try 5-6 pairs and choose the most comfortable. Try to jump, bend them as much as you can, hit the floor with heal and toe, squeeze them. Do not pay attention to the labels, your legs will hate you after 30 minutes in those pretty and “almost” comfortable boots.

Second thing you need is snowboard/ski trousers. Your favorite pair of jeans is not water resistant. The become an iceberg after 15 minutes of riding. The same thing with gloves.

Next thing is a helmet. Every snowboarder hit his head multiple times. Some of them have no brain, so it is ok. Choose your helmet the same way as boots. It should be very comfortable, almost unnoticeable. Everything else is not important.

Now you can ride comfortably with rented snowboard. It is very pleasant to go to the with a little bag and free hands. Try different sizes, different local slopes. Try to rent gear from different shops. You will notice, that 85% of riding performance is your skill. It doesn’t really matter how stiff your bindings, how stiff you board, is it rocker/camber/burton/furton… Bindings matter. Buy a good ones. They will cost you 200$+. Check that your local shop has spare parts for them.

At this moment you don’t need my advice. You will choose your deck yourself. Therefore, if you are reading this, DO NOT BUY A SNOWBOARD.
